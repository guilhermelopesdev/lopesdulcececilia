The Walcker Organ

The organ of the Great Church in Wildervank (The Netherlands) is a late romantic organ built by the Walcker organ company in 1913.

On this organ you can listen to how the German romantic music should sound. But also French and English organ music in the romantic style sounds very fine on this instrument.

In he Sixties of the last century were it was changed in an attempt to adapt to the sound of the then prevailing "neo-baroque" period.

In 2001, de Wit Organ Builders completely restored the organ. The dispostion is largely returned back to the original. The sound of the instrument is fully back into the original concept of the organ.

For more info en pictures see: www.walckerwildervank.nl
